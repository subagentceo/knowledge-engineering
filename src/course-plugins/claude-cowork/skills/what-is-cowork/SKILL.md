# What Claude Cowork Is (Chat vs. Cowork vs. Code)

> Distilled from the *Introduction to Claude Cowork* course.

Cowork is a mode of Claude inside the desktop app where Claude works on tasks *alongside* you, in your environment — not adjacent to it. You point it at a folder on your computer, connect the apps where your work lives, and describe an outcome. Claude makes a plan, works through the steps, uses the tools and files it needs, and saves a real deliverable back to your folder.

## The one mental shift that matters

**Cowork is about delegating, not just chatting.** Most new users come from Chat: type a question, see what comes back, type a follow-up, iterate. That works in Cowork too — but you get the most out of it when you reach for it for the work you'd normally **do yourself**, not the work you'd normally **ask about**. You hand Claude an entire piece of work — context-gathering, analysis, file production, tool use — and it comes back completed.

Mechanically: Chat is turn-by-turn and you stitch the steps together yourself. Cowork sustains a whole piece of work — it pulls from many tools at once, runs multi-step plans, and returns the finished output.

## Cowork works in four places

- **On your files** — reads what's in the folder you point it at, writes finished outputs back.
- **In your apps** — pulls context from email, calendar, messaging, drive, CRM you've connected.
- **In your browser** — for web tools without a connector, Claude in Chrome reads and acts on the page itself (dashboards, portals, anything behind a login).
- **With your tools** — it takes action, not just describes what to do.

## The three modes, side by side

- **Chat — thinking with Claude.** A conversation: ask, draft, brainstorm, think out loud. Best for questions, ideas, drafting something you'll polish yourself, anything that fits in a single response. Chat *can* pull from connectors, but the answer **is** the conversation.
- **Cowork — delegating to Claude.** A workspace: point at a folder + tools, describe an outcome, Claude plans/executes/delivers. Best for multi-step work across files and tools, producing real deliverables, work you set in motion and come back to.
- **Code — building software with Claude.** An agentic coding tool inside a codebase, with terminal and git access. Best for working in a repo, refactoring across source files, running tests, making commits.

To recap: **Chat is for thinking, Cowork is for delegating, Code is for software.** Most knowledge workers live in Chat and Cowork and use them for very different things.

## You stay in control

Claude shows you its plan before it starts, by default asks before actions that matter (sending, deleting, sharing), and lets you steer at any point.
