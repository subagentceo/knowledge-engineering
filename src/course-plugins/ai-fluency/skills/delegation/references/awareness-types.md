# Three types of delegation awareness

- **Problem Awareness** — get clarity on your actual goals and success metrics *before* engaging AI. Ask: What am I trying to accomplish? What does success look like? (In a learning context: "What am I trying to *learn*?" — keep the learning goal, not just the task, in view.)
- **Platform Awareness** — understand the capabilities and limitations of different AI systems so you choose the right one. A standard assistant, a reasoning-capable system, and a specialized system are not interchangeable. For learning, prefer systems that can act as tutor/coach over those that just hand back quick answers.
- **Task Delegation** — divide work to leverage each partner's strengths:
  - **Human strengths**: creativity, judgment, context, values, real-world understanding, relationships.
  - **AI strengths**: speed, consistency, information processing, breadth.
  - In learning: keep **learning-critical tasks** for yourself (e.g., constructing the argument) and delegate **support tasks** to AI (e.g., acting as a debate partner). Don't delegate away the very thing you're trying to learn.
