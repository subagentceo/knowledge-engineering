# Apply the 4Ds to disciplinary expertise

- **Discernment (quality)** — build quality criteria/rubrics, collect & annotate outstanding work, diagnose failures forensically.
- **Description (communication)** — map discipline products, reveal expert thinking (micro-decisions, flowcharts), name field norms.
- **Delegation (decomposition)** — reveal problem anatomy, map AI automation/augmentation/agent possibilities, design decision trees from cases.
- **Diligence (values)** — codify field-specific ethics ("do no harm"), clarify transparency/disclosure norms, co-create accountability policies with students.

The feedback-loop payoff: students who can articulate quality evaluate *any* output; who understand methods guide *any* process; who internalize ethics navigate *any* collaboration responsibly.
