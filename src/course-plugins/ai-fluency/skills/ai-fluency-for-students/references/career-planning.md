# AI in career planning

Core rule: **AI handles information-gathering and coaching; the human drives strategy, decisions, and values.** AI is strong at industry research, skill-gap analysis, market/salary insights, trajectory mapping, and role brainstorming; you own self-knowledge, values, trade-offs, and gut instincts.

- Make learning needs specific: not "help me learn data analysis" but "a marketing manager who needs to analyze customer surveys, starting with Excel pivot tables."
- Use AI as an interview coach (practice questions with follow-ups, feedback on content/clarity/relevance), but be **authentic** in the real thing — employers want to hire *you*, not an AI version of you. Generic AI resumes/cover letters are immediately spotted by recruiters.
- AI fluency itself is a valuable career skill to highlight. Track AI use and be transparent.
