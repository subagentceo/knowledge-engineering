# AI as a learning partner (4Ds applied to learning)

- **Delegation** — before using AI, ask "What am I trying to *learn*?" Keep learning-critical tasks (writing the argument) for yourself; delegate support tasks (debate partner). Choose AI built for learning, not quick answers.
- **Description** — AI defaults to direct answers, which is unhelpful for learning. Configure it as a tutor/coach: "Ask me questions instead of explaining," "Point me toward the solution without solving it," "Help me dig deeper into my interpretation." Useful outputs: clues, comprehension questions, critical feedback, re-explanation with new examples, progressive practice, or acting as a student you teach.
- **Discernment** — learning checks: Can I explain this to someone else? Can I solve similar problems without AI? Do I understand *why*, not just *that* it works? Cross-check AI content against non-AI sources.
- **Diligence** — follow your school's AI policy and use approved systems (creation); document interactions and disclose appropriately (transparency); be able to **explain every part of what you submit** and apply concepts to new situations (deployment).

**Living learning journal:** weekly entries on concepts worked, understanding clarity, AI usage, effective strategies, practice needs; monthly synthesis for patterns; note AI vs independent work and "aha moments."
