# Course design with the 4Ds

Three fundamental course-design tasks: (1) identify essential content/concepts, (2) map the learning journey (how topics build), (3) articulate specific learning objectives. Apply the 4Ds:

- **Delegation** — answer *What* and *Why* (content, sequencing, objectives) before *Who* and *How*; split work between your expertise and AI help.
- **Description** — create a thinking space rooted in real pedagogical challenges (e.g., "stats for journalism students who are anxious about math"); share student needs, prerequisites, outcomes, vision, and constraints. Use product and process description.
- **Discernment** — evaluate AI suggestions against your expertise: does this actually help *these* students? Explain why, don't just accept/reject.
- **Diligence** — own what reaches students; document decisions; share your reasoning with students to model responsible use.

**AI role-play technique:** ask the AI to "act as one of my students" to test course flow — where confusion arises, what background is needed at each stage, support needed for transitions.

**Learning objectives** should be accurate, meaningful, motivating, true to your teaching philosophy, and aligned with institutional requirements. Map outcomes to resume skills for student motivation. Build on context from prior collaboration (e.g., upload the syllabus).
