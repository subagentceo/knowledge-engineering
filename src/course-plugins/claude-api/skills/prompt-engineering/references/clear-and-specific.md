# Be clear and direct; be specific

## Be clear and direct

The first line is the most critical part of the prompt. Use simple, direct language: **action verb + clear task + output specs.**

- "Write three paragraphs about how solar panels work"
- "Identify three countries that use geothermal energy and for each include generation stats"
- "Generate a one-day meal plan for an athlete that meets their dietary restrictions"

(In the course this alone moved the score 2.32 → 3.92.)

## Be specific

Add guidelines to steer output. Two types:

- **Type A — attributes:** desired qualities of the output (length, structure, format). Recommended for almost all prompts; controls output characteristics.
- **Type B — steps:** specific reasoning steps for the model to follow. Use for complex problems where you want broader perspective the model wouldn't reach naturally; controls *how* it arrives at the answer.

Professional prompts often combine both. (Adding guidelines moved the course example 3.92 → 7.86.)
