## The cadence (three continuous loops + one annual)

- **WBR — Weekly Business Review.** Every week, year-round. Answers: what did customers experience last
  week? how did the business perform? are we on track to goals? "Swim in the data"; surfaces problems +
  ideas regardless of the plan.
- **PR/FAQ — Working Backwards.** Continuous, year-round. Turn an idea into an actionable initiative via
  iterated press-release + FAQ docs. Leaders read several per day; it is *not* squeezed into the planning
  window.
- **MBR / QBR.** Monthly/quarterly written reviews of progress vs. the OP plan; propose plan changes.
- **Annual planning.** Strategy (write/review/approve long-term strategy, investment envelope, OP plans)
  → Execution (audit/control throughout the year).

## Annual milestone calendar (work backward from the board review)

| step | who | when |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Top-down guidance: Metrics, Initiatives, Resources (Investment Envelope) | CEO & CFO | June (2 wk) |
| Exec offsite — vision doc(s) | CEO & exec | July (opt) |
| BU/Function offsites — vision doc(s) | leaders | Aug (opt) |
| BU/Functions write OP1 plans (Metrics, Initiatives, Resources, Org, P&L) | leaders + teams | Sept/Oct (4 wk) |
| OP1 reviews with Division Exec | leaders + exec | Oct/Nov (2 wk) |
| OP1 reviews with CEO & exec team (S-Team) | leaders + S-Team | Nov/Dec (1–4 wk) |
| Finalize OP1 P&L, decisions, goals | CEO | Nov (1 day) |
| OP1 review with the Board | CEO, CFO, BOD | Dec |
| OP2 from Q4 actuals | CEO, exec | Jan (1–2 wk) |

Fiscal=calendar: planning runs **June → January**.

## The four OP components

1. **Metrics** — financial + output metrics + the controllable **input** metrics; annual + quarterly +
   monthly goals (granularity drives precision). *Metrics first* — the one thing a leader must not delegate.
2. **Initiatives** — the work to hit the metric goals; each SMART, ideally backed by a PR/FAQ; in priority
   order; **baseline** (current resources) vs **incremental** (needs more).
3. **Resources** — OPEX (HC, marketing, SaaS) + CAPEX; annual/quarterly granularity.
4. **Org structure** — the right leaders + structure to execute (only changes if the plan demands it).

## S-Team goals

During OP1 reviews the CEO/S-Team designate ~15% of metrics/initiatives as **S-Team goals** — the most
important, tracked with owners, deliverables, dates. ~75% of S-Team meeting time goes to removing
roadblocks on these (yellow/red → green). (Bezos, 2009: 452 detailed goals for 2010.)

## OP1 document template (≈6 pages)

- **§1 Introduction** (¼ pg) — business/function, team, scope, tenets.
- **§2 Metrics** (≤1 pg) — table of 8–15 input+output metrics: prior year, current YTD, current
  plan/forecast, next-year goal, YoY%.
- **§3 Prior-year review + trends** (1–2 pg) — (a) metric commentary, (b) outcomes delivered vs plan,
  (c) wins/mistakes/misses/learnings, (d) new customer/competitor/industry trends.
- **§4 Key Initiatives** (2–3 pg) — SMART, priority order, baseline vs incremental, dependencies, PR/FAQ link.
- **§5 Resources** (≤1 pg) — HC by role/level today vs plan; non-HC fixed/variable costs.
- **Appendices** — 1: P&L · 2: initiatives table · 3: HC detail · 4: roadmap+allocation by month · 5: FAQs.

## Tips (load-bearing)

metrics first · describe problems with data (precise, not vague) · define initiatives in detail (SMART) ·
separate one-way (fixed, long-term) from two-way (variable) doors · you can control costs + initiatives,
you can *forecast* but not control revenue (hedge new-initiative revenue) · identify + commit dependencies
early · think big *and* decide what **not** to do · create customer value.
