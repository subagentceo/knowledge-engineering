<note title="Lesson 2: Applying AI Fluency to Course Design and Learning Outcomes">
**Lesson 2: Applying AI Fluency to Course Design and Learning Outcomes**

**Core Concept**
AI Fluency transforms course design by building rich, shared context that turns AI from generic assistant into collaborative partner who understands specific teaching situation, constraints, and pedagogical vision.

**Three Fundamental Course Design Tasks**
1. Identifying content and concepts essential for students
2. Mapping learning journey - how topics build on each other
3. Articulating specific learning objectives

**4Ds Applied to Course Design**

**Delegation in Course Design**
- Make smart delegation decisions based on subject matter expertise and goals
- Determine what work needs to be done (content selection, sequencing, objectives)
- Decide who does what (human, AI, or both) and how
- Answer What and Why before determining Who and How

**Description for Course Design**
- Create thinking space rooted in actual pedagogical challenges
- Example: "I'm building stats course for journalism students who need data literacy but often come anxious about math or skeptical statistics matter for careers"
- Share specific challenges, student needs, prerequisites, outcomes
- Explain vision for success and constraints
- Use both product description (what you want AI to do) and process description (how)

**Product Discernment in Course Design**
- Evaluate AI suggestions against own expertise
- Question: Does this concept actually help target students?
- Don't just accept/reject - explain why something works/doesn't in context
- Use expertise to shape better future suggestions
- Recognize unexpected insights and new thinking approaches

**Diligence in Course Design**
- Don't outsource decisions - take responsibility for what reaches students
- Document key decisions in AI collaboration
- Share thinking with students to model responsible AI use
- Quality assurance and transparency
- Maintain accountability while demonstrating how AI enhances teaching expertise

**AI Role-Playing Technique**
Ask AI to "act as one of my students" to evaluate course flow:
- How will students react at different points?
- Where might confusion arise?
- What background needed at each stage?
- What support needed for topic transitions?

**Learning Objectives Enhancement**
- Use established context from previous collaboration
- Upload course syllabus with richer context description
- Craft objectives that are: accurate, meaningful, motivating, true to teaching philosophy, aligned with institutional requirements
- Map course outcomes to resume skills for student motivation
- Experiment with different approaches due to faster process

**Benefits Beyond Time-Saving**
- Surface hidden assumptions and blindspots
- Discover creative connections between concepts
- Maintain coherence while exploring new ideas
- Build personal competencies that strengthen with practice

**Key Principles**
- 4Ds = framework for genuine cognitive partnership, not checklist
- Pedagogical and subject expertise guides collaborative exploration
- Context-building approach works for: new courses, course updates, curriculum sequences, learning pathways
- Start with big picture, build/maintain context through details
- Goal = enhancement not just efficiency - better teaching/learning, not just faster planning

**Exercise Structure (50 minutes)**
Stage 1: Planning approach (clarify course, work needed, expertise vs AI help)
Stage 2: Initiating collaboration (share teaching context, explain challenges, set partnership)
Stage 3: Build conversation through Description & Discernment (content identification, journey mapping, learning objectives)
Stage 4: Documentation & reflection (verify accuracy, document decisions, note rejections, create AI role statement)

**Outcome**
Creates genuine cognitive partnership where human judgment, values, and student relationships remain central while AI enhances pedagogical practice.

<note title="Lesson 1: Introduction to AI Fluency for Educators">
AI Fluency for Educators = framework for effective, efficient, ethical, safe AI integration in teaching practice

Course Purpose = applying 4D Framework (Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence) specifically to education contexts

Why AI Fluency Matters in Education:
- Students already using AI
- Employers expect AI fluency from graduates  
- Institutions need responsible AI integration strategies
- Educators shape next generation's AI engagement

Course Outcomes:
- Apply 4D Framework to teaching practice
- Use AI for course design and lesson planning
- Create materials maintaining teaching vision and academic integrity
- Lead institutional AI conversations
- Model responsible AI engagement for students

Prerequisites = completion of AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course

4D FRAMEWORK SUMMARY:

AI Fluency = responsible human in the loop, augmentation not automation

Delegation = determining who does what work (human vs AI vs collaborative)
Three types:
- Problem Awareness = clarity on goals and success metrics before AI engagement
- Platform Awareness = understanding different AI capabilities and limitations  
- Task Delegation = dividing work to leverage human creativity/judgment + AI speed/processing

Description = how to communicate with AI (conversation not commands)
Three dimensions:
- Product Description = detailed specifications for final output (length, audience, style, format)
- Process Description = guiding AI's approach (step-by-step thinking, multiple perspectives)
- Performance Description = defining AI behavior during interaction (critical editor, supportive brainstormer)

Discernment = evaluating AI outputs and adjusting accordingly
Three levels:
- Product Discernment = quality assessment of AI creations (accuracy, helpfulness, new perspectives)
- Process Discernment = examining AI reasoning and assumptions
- Performance Discernment = evaluating AI behavior during interaction

Description + Discernment = continuous feedback loop transforming AI from tool to thinking partner

Diligence = responsible AI use throughout collaboration
Three aspects:
- Creation Diligence = thoughtful AI system selection considering privacy, security, appropriateness
- Transparency Diligence = honest disclosure of AI assistance
- Deployment Diligence = taking ownership and responsibility for AI-assisted outputs

Key Principles:
- Augmentation > Automation (especially in learning contexts)
- Pedagogical values remain central
- 4Ds work together as interconnected competencies
- Skills remain relevant as AI technology evolves

Teaching Context Document = reusable artifact establishing shared understanding with AI for future collaborations

Essential Context Areas:
- Subject area, level, specific courses
- Student backgrounds, challenges, goals
- Institutional constraints
- Teaching philosophy and methods
- Common teaching pain points
- AI integration goals
- Unique teaching situation aspects

Context Document Benefits:
- Improves AI collaboration effectiveness
- Saves time in future interactions
- Maintains consistency across AI conversations
- Helps AI understand educator's specific needs and constraints

<note title="Lesson 3: Applying AI Fluency to Learning Materials and Assignments">
**LESSON 3: APPLYING AI FLUENCY TO LEARNING MATERIALS AND ASSIGNMENTS**

**CORE LEARNING OBJECTIVES**
- Build teaching materials with AI using 4D framework
- Leverage established context for coherent material development  
- Apply systematic quality control through Discernment
- Create integrated learning experiences

**AI'S DISCIPLINARY IMPACT FRAMEWORK**

**Three Critical Questions for Disciplines:**
1. What gets automated = routine tasks likely automated by AI in future careers
2. Partnership potential = where human-AI collaboration most impactful
3. Who's in charge = how humans manage/remain accountable for AI work

**Three Types of Existing Expertise:**
- Disciplinary Expertise = field content, values, methods, thinking patterns
- Pedagogical Expertise = student struggle points, breakthrough moments, mastery development
- Assessment Expertise = recognizing genuine understanding, designing evaluations

**AI DISRUPTION ACROSS THREE DOMAINS**

**Curriculum Impact:**
- Fundamentals remain but students question necessity
- Need compelling discipline-specific answers for "why learn this when AI can do it"
- Tasks: Identify what AI can automate, determine which concepts become more/less important, assess how AI changes best practices

**Pedagogical Impact:**
- AI enables personalized tutoring, interactive simulations, immediate feedback
- Challenge: distinguishing AI that enhances vs shortcuts learning
- Tasks: Define how AI enhances/inhibits learning, identify best teacher-student-AI collaborations, determine effective pedagogical approaches

**Assessment Impact:**
- Most urgent disruption = when students generate content in seconds, what are we assessing
- Need authentic assessment of both AI-assisted and human-only work
- Tasks: Identify authentic demonstrations of AI-assisted learning, create shortcut-proof assignments, value process over products, adapt to inevitable student AI use

**4Ds APPLIED TO DISCIPLINARY EXPERTISE**

**Discernment = Quality Evaluation**
- Build Quality Criteria = detailed rubrics beyond vague terms, specific excellence markers
- Collect Outstanding Work = systematic analysis with students, make expert thinking visible
- Diagnose Failures = forensic examination of flawed examples, understand failure modes

**Description = Communication Mastery**  
- Map Discipline Products = document key outputs with precision, reveal underlying logic
- Reveal Expert Thinking = make problem-solving processes visible, trace micro-decisions
- Name Norms = surface field-specific behaviors, define "thinking like X" in concrete terms

**Delegation = Work Decomposition**
- Reveal Problem Anatomy = break challenges into components, map AI possibilities
- Map AI Possibilities = what can be automated/augmented/delegated to AI agents
- Design Decision Trees = frameworks for when/how to involve AI, case-study based

**Diligence = Ethical Standards**
- Codify Ethical Frameworks = field-specific "do no harm," create decision matrices
- Clarify Transparency Norms = disclosure expectations, citation templates for AI assistance  
- Co-Create Accountability Policies = build standards with students, develop peer review protocols

**MATERIAL CREATION WITH AI**

**Context Utilization:**
- Established context = each new workflow better than starting fresh
- Reference previous conversations and course design work
- Share video transcripts and course context with AI

**4Ds in Material Creation:**
- Delegation = understand what materials needed and why
- Description = leverage established context + specific requirements
- Description-Discernment Loop = refinement tool, explain why suggestions work/don't work
- Diligence = protect sensitive data, verify accuracy, check bias, create transparency

**Four Material Creation Options:**

**Option 1: Lecture Slide Deck**
- Setup: Share course context, presentation style, classroom dynamics, visual preferences
- Process: Initial outline → evaluate flow/complexity → develop crucial slides → full deck
- Final check: Verify accuracy, note image/citation needs, document AI contribution

**Option 2: Study/Revision Guide**  
- Setup: Reference lecture topic, describe student study habits, explain effective aids
- Process: Identify key concepts → develop self-check questions → anticipate misconceptions → create connections
- Final check: Verify answer accuracy, check alignment with assessments, ensure accessibility

**Option 3: Interactive In-Class Exercise**
- Setup: Describe classroom setup, time constraints, student interaction patterns, learning goals
- Process: Collaborate on activity → think through logistics → anticipate responses → create facilitation notes
- Final check: Test instruction clarity, consider accessibility, plan contingencies

**Option 4: Knowledge Check Quiz**
- Setup: Reference learning objectives, assessment level, academic integrity approach
- Process: Develop 5-10 varied questions → mix question types → connect to objectives → create answer explanations  
- Final check: Verify answer correctness, check for bias/confusion, ensure appropriate difficulty

**KEY PRINCIPLES**
- AI Fluency = amplifying human expertise, not replacing it
- Disciplinary knowledge = foundation for unprecedented achievement  
- Human capabilities = domain expertise, judgment, creativity, ethics, relationships
- Students prepared to be irreplaceable, not replaced
- Focus on coherence and quality, not just efficiency
- Description-Discernment loop = powerful refinement through meaningful iteration
