<note title="Introduction and Approaches to Teaching AI Fluency">
AI FLUENCY FRAMEWORK = dual-purpose tool that describes what happens when people work with AI AND guides them toward better practices

COURSE STRUCTURE = 3 lessons addressing: 1) How to introduce framework to students (pedagogy), 2) How to assess if students understand (assessment), 3) How to integrate with existing curriculum

TEACHING APPROACHES = 4 distinct entry points for introducing AI Fluency Framework to students

APPROACH 1: STEP-BY-STEP (LINEAR)
- Framework as sequential process: Delegation → Description → Discernment → Diligence
- Best for: Beginning AI users who need structure and manageable progression
- Implementation: Four-part assignments building sequentially (e.g., part 1 = Delegation/project planning, part 2 = Description/hands-on workshops, part 3 = Discernment/evaluation, part 4 = Diligence/ethics)
- Benefits: Natural, intuitive, creates structured learning journey

APPROACH 2: START ANYWHERE (NON-LINEAR) 
- Framework as interconnected system where any competency can be starting point
- Best for: Experienced AI users who can handle real-world complexity
- Key insight: Competencies inform each other (Discernment problems → new Delegation decisions; Diligence requirements → different Description strategies; Description challenges → Delegation gaps)
- Implementation: Group projects, case-based learning with real AI collaboration scenarios
- Benefits: Reflects messy realities of actual AI collaboration, builds adaptive thinking

APPROACH 3: JUST ONE D (FOCUSED)
- Deep exploration of single competency at a time
- Best for: Specialized purposes, skill-building sessions, workshops
- Implementation: Entire workshop on one competency (e.g., three Discernment sessions covering Product/Process/Performance evaluation)
- Benefits: Masters specific area without cognitive overload, allows nuanced exploration of techniques and edge cases

APPROACH 4: TWO LOOPS
- Most conceptually rich approach treating AI fluency as nested processes
- Best for: Students who grasp basics and need deeper understanding
- Structure: Delegation-Diligence loop (strategic/ethical decisions) + Description-Discernment loop (tactical/iterative work)
- Benefits: Reveals how competencies constantly inform each other in practice

DELEGATION-DILIGENCE LOOP = strategic and ethical decision-making framework for responsible AI collaboration

LOOP COMPONENTS:
Delegation = Problem Awareness + Platform Awareness + Task Delegation
Diligence = Creation Diligence + Transparency Diligence + Deployment Diligence

BIDIRECTIONAL FLOW:
Forward (Delegation → Diligence): Strategic decisions raise ethical questions
Reverse (Diligence → Delegation): Ethical constraints clarify and improve strategic choices

TEACHING STRATEGIES:
- Applied scenarios making connections visible
- Step-by-step guides for both directions
- Recognition that constraints enhance rather than limit creativity
- Focus on developing clear rationales for choices

DESCRIPTION-DISCERNMENT LOOP = moment-to-moment craft of building cognitive environments for human-AI collaboration

CORE CONCEPT: Conversations not commands - building shared context and understanding rather than single prompt exchanges

COGNITIVE ENVIRONMENT = collaborative context including:
- Shared vocabulary and references evolving over time
- Well-defined goals, values, processes, methods
- Established interaction patterns enabling optimal performance
- Mechanisms for building on previous interactions

THREE LENSES:
PRODUCT = what we're creating together (evolving understanding of goals and quality)
PROCESS = shared approaches to thinking and problem-solving
PERFORMANCE = relationship dynamics between human and AI

TEACHING STRATEGIES:
- Multi-interaction assignments requiring context accumulation
- Share instructor's own AI collaboration processes
- Document evolution of collaboration, not just outputs
- Move beyond automation to augmentation

SUCCESS INDICATORS:
- Shared Language: Shorthand references to complex ideas from earlier conversations
- Exploration Mindset: Evolution from rigid commands to flexible, interactive approaches

NESTED SYSTEMS = how the two loops work together:
Delegation-Diligence = strategic container setting direction and boundaries
Description-Discernment = tactical content filling container with rich interaction
Result = Responsible, sophisticated AI collaboration transcending what either partner achieves alone

LESSON DURATION = 2 hours (30 minutes video, 90 minutes exercises)

EXERCISE STRUCTURE = 3 progressive exercises building teaching capability:
1) Establish teaching context and explore approaches (30 min)
2) Design Delegation-Diligence loop lesson (30 min) 
3) Design Description-Discernment loop lesson (30 min)

# Assessing and Designing AI Fluency Notes

## Core Assessment Framework

AI Fluency Assessment = Outcome-based + Process-based + Reflection-based approaches combined

**Outcome-based assessment** = Focus on what students produce through AI collaboration. Assess whether students achieved stated goals through human-AI partnership.

**Process-based assessment** = Examine how students work with AI over time. Captures iteration patterns, recovery from failures, methodological sophistication.

**Reflection-based assessment** = Focus on metacognitive awareness. Students analyze why strategies worked/didn't work, what they learned, future applications.

## Assessing the 4Ds

### Delegation Assessment
- **Outcome**: Did delegation plan make sense? Were goals realistic? Right tool selection?
- **Process**: Review annotated chat logs showing exploration of options and decision-making
- **Reflection**: Students explain choices, alternatives considered, how delegation shaped outcomes

### Description Assessment  
- **Outcome**: Quality of prompts and AI responses. Clear instructions? Good context? Evolution from initial to final versions
- **Process**: Conversation logs showing iterative refinement, failed approaches, building shared context
- **Reflection**: Analysis of which description techniques worked best, task-specific communication approaches, description quality vs output quality relationship

### Discernment Assessment
- **Outcome**: Students annotate AI outputs marking strengths/weaknesses. Decision logs explaining what was kept/modified/discarded
- **Process**: In-line evaluation comments, catching/correcting errors, pattern recognition across interactions
- **Reflection**: Analysis of evaluation criteria evolution, initially missed issues, comparison across task types

### Diligence Assessment
- **Outcome**: Quality of diligence statements, attribution/transparency documentation, fact-checking evidence
- **Process**: Data handling in chat logs, ethical decision-making, sensitive information handling, constraint-checking
- **Reflection**: Discussion of ethical dilemmas, responsibility understanding, surprising challenges, future improvements

## Assignment Design Principles

**Authenticity** = Create assignments mirroring real-world AI collaboration, genuine problems where AI partnership adds value

**Iteration** = Build in refinement opportunities showcasing growth over time

**Pedagogical Transparency** = Clear assessment of collaboration process and reflections, not just outputs

## Assignment Types

### Outcome-Based Assignments
- **Improving AI outputs**: Transform mediocre AI output into excellent work
- **Product comparison**: Use multiple AI systems for same task, analyze differences
- **Constraint-based challenges**: Specific requirements (format, length, style, audience)
- **Peer product review**: Students set goals, create with AI, swap and critique

### Process-Based Assignments
- **Annotated chat logs**: Students highlight turning points, breakthroughs, failure recovery
- **Recorded narrations**: Real-time decision-making explanation during AI work
- **Process playbooks**: Personal AI strategy guides for different task types
- **AI-assisted/peer debrief**: Discussion of chat logs with AI or peer partners

### Reflection-Based Assignments
- **Guided inquiry**: Specific questions about particular assignments
- **Learning journal**: Self-assess 4D development with evidence across course
- **Scenarios/case studies**: Apply learnings to hypothetical or real-world situations
- **Personal policy statements**: Develop own values/strategies for AI collaboration

## Managing Assignment Volume

**Detailed deliverable-based rubrics** = Clear, granular deliverables for quick verification

**Self and peer review emphasis** = Students best positioned to observe development with proper guidance

**Lightning round conferences** = Brief conferences replacing written feedback (5-minute discussions)

**Selective sampling** = Don't read every word; students flag key moments for attention

## Key Assessment Artifacts

**Chat logs** = Primary process documentation
**Diligence statements** = Transparency and attribution documentation
**Learning journals** = Reflection and metacognitive development
**Decision logs** = Record of what AI output was kept/modified/discarded
**Process playbooks** = Personal strategy documentation

## Assessment Focus Areas

Focus on **observable actions and concrete artifacts** rather than assumptions about understanding

Different competencies benefit from different assessment approaches

Assessment should be **learning opportunity, not just measurement**

Students need to understand you care about **how they learn to work with AI**, not just what they produce

Essential principle: Make invisible decision-making visible through documentation and reflection

<note title="AI Fluency Assignment Component Guide">
AI FLUENCY ASSIGNMENT COMPONENTS

OUTCOME-BASED COMPONENTS

Improving AI Outputs = Students transform mediocre AI output into excellent work through critique and iteration
- Assessment focus: Discernment, Description
- Deliverables: Original with annotations + improved version
- Effective because: Forces explicit quality standards, requires domain knowledge application, develops AI guidance skills
- Variations: Different flaw types, peer improvement exchanges

Product Comparison = Students use multiple AI systems for same task, analyze differences
- Assessment focus: Delegation, Discernment  
- Deliverables: Multiple outputs + comparison matrix + recommendation report
- Effective because: Builds platform awareness, creates evidence-based selection skills, reveals trade-offs
- Process: Use standard AI assistant + reasoning-capable system + specialized system

Constraint-Based Challenges = Achieve specific requirements through AI collaboration within limits
- Assessment focus: Description, Delegation
- Deliverables: Final product meeting all constraints
- Effective because: Develops precise AI communication, reveals specification importance, builds problem-solving under limitations
- Variations: Ethical constraints, conflicting constraints, time pressure

Peer Product Review = Evaluate AI-assisted work by peers against goals and ethics
- Assessment focus: Discernment, Diligence
- Deliverables: Review feedback form
- Criteria: Goal achievement, appropriate AI use, human oversight quality, AI attribution adequacy
- Variations: Blind review, group sessions

PROCESS-BASED COMPONENTS

Annotated Chat Logs = Submit complete chat logs with detailed annotations
- Assessment focus: Description, Discernment
- Annotations mark: Turning points, insights/breakthroughs, failures/recovery, communication evolution, shared context development
- Effective because: Makes thinking visible, captures authentic problem-solving, reveals communication evolution
- Variations: Competency-focused annotations, pattern comparisons

Recorded Narrations = Screen recording with real-time audio narration of AI work
- Assessment focus: All 4Ds
- Narration covers: Choice rationale, response evaluation, alternatives considered, ethical considerations
- Effective because: Captures authentic decision-making, reveals tacit knowledge, shows actual vs reported work
- Variations: Paired commentary, before/after comparisons

Process Playbooks = Personal reference guides for AI collaboration strategies
- Assessment focus: All 4Ds
- Includes: Conversation guides, decision trees, quality checklists, pitfall recovery, ethical guidelines
- Effective because: Encourages systematic documentation, creates useful resource, builds metacognitive awareness
- Variations: Collaborative playbooks, discipline-specific requirements

AI-Assisted/Peer-Assisted Debrief = Structured analysis of AI collaboration with external perspective
- Assessment focus: All 4Ds
- Analysis covers: Positive patterns, missed opportunities, strengths, improvement areas
- Effective because: Provides external perspective, reveals blind spots, encourages peer learning

REFLECTION-BASED COMPONENTS

Guided Inquiry = Respond to specific questions promoting deep reflection
- Assessment focus: Variable by questions
- Question types: Surprise moments, failed strategies, communication evolution, ethical decisions
- Effective because: Targets specific AI fluency aspects, requires evidence-based reflection
- Variations: Discipline-tailored, progressive questions

Learning Journal = Ongoing documentation of AI fluency development
- Assessment focus: All 4Ds over time
- Weekly entries: Attempts, successes, challenges, skill improvements, future goals
- Monthly synthesis: Pattern identification, goal setting
- Variations: Multimedia journals, collaborative journals, AI conversation journals

Scenarios and Case Studies = Apply AI fluency learning to realistic scenarios
- Assessment focus: Diligence, Delegation
- Analysis includes: Delegation decisions, ethical considerations, action plans, risk mitigation
- Effective because: Develops strategic thinking, builds ethical reasoning, connects theory to practice
- Variations: Real-world cases, discipline-specific scenarios, stakeholder role-play

Personal Policy Statements = Synthesize learning into personal AI collaboration framework
- Assessment focus: Diligence, All 4Ds holistically
- Components: Ethical principles, decision criteria, quality standards, transparency commitments, boundaries, learning strategies
- Effective because: Requires deep synthesis, builds professional identity, creates accountability
- Variations: Collaborative policies for group work

ASSESSMENT MATRICES

Delegation Assessment Matrix:
- Strong performance = Strategic thinking about when/why/how to collaborate with AI
- Outcome focus: Task division quality, tool selection appropriateness, goal achievement
- Process focus: Decision documentation, platform exploration, adaptation evidence
- Reflection focus: Choice analysis, alternative consideration, impact evaluation

Description Assessment Matrix:
- Strong performance = Effective AI communication regarding outputs/processes/behaviors
- Outcome focus: Prompt evolution, context building, communication clarity
- Process focus: Iterative refinement, recovery patterns, shared vocabulary development
- Reflection focus: Strategy analysis, technique recognition, context insights

Discernment Assessment Matrix:
- Strong performance = Critical evaluation using domain-specific, nuanced criteria
- Outcome focus: Annotation quality, evaluation depth, evidence-based judgment
- Process focus: Error catching, consistency, quality improvement over time
- Reflection focus: Criteria evolution, missed issue analysis, meta-evaluation skills

Diligence Assessment Matrix:
- Strong performance = Integrated ethical reasoning throughout collaboration
- Outcome focus: Attribution quality, transparency documentation, verification evidence
- Process focus: Data handling, permission documentation, constraint checking
- Reflection focus: Ethical dilemma discussion, responsibility understanding, values articulation

Matrix Usage:
- Rubric development: Select relevant Ds, choose assessment types, adapt indicators, define performance levels
- Feedback provision: Use indicators to guide review, reference strong performance markers
- Student assessment: Share criteria beforehand, encourage self-identification of competency evidence

<note title="AIs Impact on Your Discipline">
**LESSON OVERVIEW**
- Duration = 50+ minutes (20 min video + 30 min exercises + offline discussion)
- Focus = AI impacts on specific disciplines across curriculum/pedagogy/assessment
- Goal = Create discipline-specific AI fluency applications using 4Ds framework

**CORE PRINCIPLE**
AI Fluency = Amplifying human expertise, not replacing it
Disciplinary knowledge = Foundation for unprecedented achievement through AI partnership

**THREE KEY QUESTIONS FOR GRADUATES**
1. What gets automated? = Which routine career tasks will AI handle
2. Partnership potential = Where human-AI collaboration most impactful  
3. Who's in charge? = How to manage/remain accountable for independent AI work

**AI DISRUPTION PATTERNS**
Disruption ≠ uniform across disciplines
- Curriculum may stay stable while assessments transform
- Pedagogy might evolve while core content constant
- Some disruptions = opportunities to leverage
- Others = problems to solve
- Your expertise determines which is which

**THREE EXPERTISE TYPES FOR AI RESPONSE**
1. Disciplinary Expertise = Field content, values, methods, thinking patterns
2. Pedagogical Expertise = Student struggle/breakthrough patterns, learning emotional journey
3. Assessment Expertise = Recognizing genuine understanding, designing deep learning evaluations

**CURRICULUM IMPACT ANALYSIS**
Tasks for discipline assessment:
- Identify AI automation capabilities in field
- Determine foundational concepts importance shifts with AI
- Map AI augmentation improvements to best practices
- Develop responses to "Why learn X when AI does it?" questions
- Define how to direct AI agents effectively

**PEDAGOGY TRANSFORMATION**
Enhancement opportunities:
- Personalized tutoring at scale
- Interactive simulations  
- Immediate feedback systems
- AI as educational assistant/interactive learning partner

Risk assessment:
- Distinguish AI that enhances vs diminishes learning
- Identify teacher-student AI collaborations that leverage potential
- Determine pedagogical approaches to embrace vs avoid
- Address reality that students will use AI regardless

**ASSESSMENT REVOLUTION** 
Core challenges:
- When students generate essays in seconds, what are we assessing?
- Create evaluations honoring individual growth/creativity/problem-solving
- Assess both AI-assisted and human-only performances

Essential tasks:
- Identify authentic learning demonstration in AI-assisted work
- Create assignments students can't shortcut with AI
- Value process/growth, not just products
- Determine where AI collaboration enhances vs undermines objectives
- Adapt to student AI use reality

**IRREPLACEABLE HUMAN CAPABILITIES**
- Domain expertise
- Real-world context understanding
- Judgment in ambiguous situations
- Creative problem-solving
- Ethical reasoning
- Relationship building

**APPLYING 4DS TO DISCIPLINES**

**DISCERNMENT = Quality evaluation ability**
Build Quality Criteria:
- Articulate excellence beyond vague terms
- Create detailed rubrics capturing deep quality markers
- Document criteria students can internalize/apply

Collect Outstanding Work:
- Analyze exemplars systematically with students
- Make expert thinking visible through annotation guides

Diagnose Failures:
- Study flawed examples forensically
- Understand failure modes for working alone or with AI

**DESCRIPTION = Field communication mastery**
Map Discipline Products:
- Document key field outputs with precision
- Create templates revealing underlying logic
- Have students reverse-engineer professional outputs

Reveal Expert Thinking:
- Make expert problem-approach processes visible
- Document micro-decisions and iterations
- Create flowcharts of expert thinking

Name Field Norms:
- Surface behaviors defining your field
- Build behavioral performance modes with students

**DELEGATION = Work decomposition understanding**
Reveal Problem Anatomy:
- Break challenges into component parts
- Create problem anatomy diagrams
- Map AI automation/augmentation/agent possibilities

Design Decision Trees:
- Create frameworks for when/how to involve AI
- Build through case studies making delegation decisions explicit

**DILIGENCE = Field values embodiment**
Codify Ethical Frameworks:
- Define field-specific "do no harm"
- Build case studies exploring ethical edges
- Create ethical decision matrices

Clarify Transparency Norms:
- Document disclosure expectations by field
- Create AI assistance disclosure templates

Co-Create Accountability Policies:
- Build standards with students, not impose them
- Draft policies together
- Develop peer review protocols

**FEEDBACK LOOP BENEFITS**
Students who articulate quality → better evaluate any output
Students who understand methods → guide any process effectively  
Students who internalize ethics → navigate any collaboration responsibly

**FINAL MESSAGE**
Goal ≠ preparing students to be replaced by AI
Goal = preparing students to be irreplaceable
Future needs humans who: think critically, communicate clearly, collaborate wisely, act responsibly

Human expertise + AI fluency = Foundation for thriving in AI-enhanced future
